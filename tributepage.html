Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

American minister and civil rights activist


Here's a time line of Dr. King Jr.'s life

  • 1929. Born on 15th January into a Baptist home, second of the
    three children to Reverend Michael King Sr. and Alberta King.

  • 1935. Started school. Had to attend an elementary school for
    black children (due to racism) while his white friends went to
    a separate school for white children.

  • 1940. Enrolled in Atlanta University Laboratory School for his
    seventh grade. While there, he took piano and violin lessons
    and showed keen interests in History and English classes.

  • 1942. Became the youngest assistant manager of a newspaper
    delivery station for the Atlanta Journal.

  • 1944. Gave his first public speech during an oratorical contest,
    which he won. In his speech he stated, black America still
    wears chains. The finest negro is at the mercy of the meanest
    white man. Even winners of our highest honors face the class
    color bar.     Passed his entrance examination into Morehouse
    College     in the same year.

  • 1947. He chose to enter the ministry, after studying under the
    mentorship of Baptist minister Benjamin Mays .

  • 1948. Graduated from Morehouse with a Bachelor of Arts (BA)
    in sociology aged nineteen.

  • 1951. Graduated with a Bachelor of Divinity degree from
    Crozer Theological Seminary. Began doctoral studies in systematic
    theology at Boston University while he worked as an assistant
    minister at Boston's historic Twelfth Baptist Church.

  • 1953. Married Coretta Scott on June 18. They became the parents
    of four children.

  • 1954. Was called as pastor of the Dexter Avenue Baptist Church
    in Montgomery, Alabama.

  • 1955. Received his Ph.D. degree on June 5.

  • 1959. After being based in Montgomery for five years, he
    returned to Atlanta where he served until his death as co-pastor
    with his father at Ebenezer Baptist Church.
